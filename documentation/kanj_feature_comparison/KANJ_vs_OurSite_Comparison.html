KANJ website: https://www.kanj.org/ (Kerala Association of New Jersey)
Our site: MOSC community / event platform (this repository)
Document date: May 19, 2026

This page summarizes observed UX and feature differences between KANJ.org and our site, with emphasis on gaps we can close and areas where our platform already leads (events, ticketing backend, richer homepage, admin tools).

Use the Key differences you noticed section below as the primary checklist; later sections add context, recommendations, and strengths.

Key differences you noticed

The following seven items are the main comparison points called out for product and design review. Each has its own section with behavior notes and suggested direction for our site.

  1. Social sharing options for events (KANJ yes, ours no)
  2. All upcoming event details in a single click — card rotate on KANJ
  3. Graphics are very minimal on KANJ
  4. Event ticket buy pages are very modern on KANJ — we can improve ours
  5. Sponsor details on homepage (ours) vs separate sponsors page button (KANJ)
  6. “Send us a message” on KANJ vs contact section only on ours
  7. Chat widget on KANJ, not on ours

1. Event social sharing

Gap — share event to social networks
KANJ.org Our site
Users can share an event through social sharing options (spread event links on Facebook, X/Twitter, etc., or native share where supported). Event pages and listings do not currently expose a dedicated “Share this event” control or pre-filled social URLs.

Why it matters

Community events grow through word of mouth. One-click sharing reduces friction for members promoting workshops, galas, and fundraisers.

Suggested direction for our site

  • Add a share cluster on /events/[id] and on featured/upcoming cards: copy link, Facebook, X, WhatsApp (common for diaspora communities).
  • Use Open Graph / Twitter Card meta tags so shared links show title, date, and image.
  • Optionally use the Web Share API on mobile when the browser supports it.

Related code areas: src/app/events/[id]/page.tsx, FeaturedEventsSection.tsx, UpcomingEventsSection.tsx.

2. Upcoming events — one-click card detail (rotate)

Gap — instant detail without leaving the list
KANJ.org Our site
On upcoming events, one click on the card can rotate or flip the card to show full details (date, time, location, register) without a full page load. Users typically open a separate event page or scroll through a longer card; no single-click flip interaction on homepage upcoming strips.

Why it matters

Reduces steps for users comparing several events on a phone. KANJ optimizes for quick scanning; our site optimizes for rich detail pages (which is good for SEO and deep content but slower for browse-only visits).

Suggested direction for our site

  • Prototype a 3D flip or back-face reveal on upcoming event cards (CSS transform or Framer Motion), with “View full page” on the back.
  • Ensure keyboard accessibility: flip on Enter, focus trap optional, respect prefers-reduced-motion.
  • Keep deep links to /events/[id] for sharing and admin consistency.

3. Graphics and visual density

Design philosophy difference
KANJ.org Our site
Very minimal graphics — white space, simple cards, professional association look; fewer decorative elements. Rich visual design — hero split layout, particle background, purple glass cards, gradients, animated sections, sponsor imagery on homepage.

Takeaway

Neither approach is wrong. KANJ reads as a traditional nonprofit; our site reads as a modern event brand. If leadership wants closer parity with KANJ:

  • Offer a simplified homepage variant or reduce motion/graphics on key listing pages.
  • Keep rich design on marketing landing areas but use flatter layouts on /events list views.

See also: HeroSection.tsx, HomeParticleBackground.tsx, homepage glass card styles in globals.css.

4. Ticket purchase pages

Opportunity — modernize checkout UX
KANJ.org Our site
Ticket / registration flows feel modern: clear steps, minimal clutter, mobile-friendly buy path aligned with current event-site patterns. We have Stripe integration, ticket types, QR/check-in in admin, and mobile payment flows — strong backend; the public ticket page UI can be refreshed to match contemporary checkout (progress, summary sidebar, trust cues).

Suggested direction for our site

  • Audit checkout from event detail → ticket selection → payment → confirmation; benchmark against KANJ and leading event platforms.
  • Improve typography, spacing, and step indicators; show order summary and fees clearly (see numerical/currency display rules in codebase).
  • Test Payment Request Button / wallet flows on mobile (already documented in project rules).

Related code areas: Stripe routes under src/app/api/stripe/, event checkout components, ticket type admin.

5. Sponsors — homepage vs dedicated page

Information architecture difference
KANJ.org Our site
Sponsors are reached via a separate navigation item (e.g. “Sponsors”) and a dedicated sponsors page with full sponsor details. Sponsor details appear on the homepage via OurSponsorsSection — high visibility for partners; may crowd homepage or duplicate content if a full sponsors page is also needed.

Suggested direction for our site

  • Keep homepage sponsor strip for visibility (good for sponsors).
  • Add a header/footer link “All sponsors” → /sponsors or /gallery-style page with tiers, logos, and contact for sponsorship.
  • Align with KANJ pattern: homepage = teaser, dedicated page = full directory.

6. “Send us a message” vs contact section

Gap — structured messaging service
KANJ.org Our site
“Send us a message” — a distinct service or form flow for inquiries (often routed to the right committee or inbox). Contact section on homepage: email, phone, address, social links — but no equivalent branded “message us” workflow with topic routing or ticket-style follow-up.

KANJ also offers (for reference)

Multiple department emails (president@, kidsclub@, careercounseling@, etc.). Our site has general contact info; expanding to topic-based routing would narrow the gap.

Suggested direction for our site

  • Add /contact or enhance Contact block with a form: name, email, topic (Events, Membership, Sponsorship, Technical), message.
  • Backend: email via SES/SendGrid or store in admin “inquiries” queue.
  • Mirror KANJ label: “Send us a message” as primary CTA.

Related code areas: HomePageClient.tsx contact section, src/app/contact if present.

7. Live chat widget

Gap — real-time chat on public site
KANJ.org Our site
Chat widget embedded (third-party live chat or bot) for immediate questions during browsing. No chat widget on public pages; users rely on email, phone, or contact form only.

Suggested direction for our site

  • Evaluate providers (Intercom, Crisp, Tawk.to, Facebook Messenger plugin) for cost and volunteer staffing.
  • If chat is not staffed, use async alternatives: FAQ + “Send us a message” with SLA text (“We reply within 2 business days”).
  • Load widget only after consent if required by privacy policy; keep Clerk/middleware ignoredRoutes in mind for script domains.

What our site does well (vs KANJ)

Strengths to preserve while closing gaps
  • Event platform depth — Ticket types, Stripe checkout, webhooks, admin event/media/ticket management, QR/check-in patterns.
  • Engaging homepage — Hero split, featured/live/upcoming events, team, causes, testimonials, cultural workshops, particle + glass UI (recent redesign).
  • Multi-tenant / admin — Clerk auth, tenant-scoped admin, manage events/usage (KANJ is primarily a marketing + registration site).
  • Polls — Interactive polling (admin + public patterns in repo); not a KANJ public feature.
  • Media-rich event pages — Galleries, sponsors on event detail, discount codes (admin).
  • Sponsor visibility — Homepage sponsor section gives partners immediate exposure (complement with a full sponsors page rather than removing).

Recommended priorities

Suggested implementation order
  • Event social sharing + OG meta tags on event pages
  • “Send us a message” form with topic routing
  • Ticket/checkout UI refresh (modern step flow)
  • Dedicated sponsors page + nav link (keep homepage strip)
  • Upcoming event card flip or inline expand on homepage
  • Chat widget or documented async alternative
  • Optional simplified visual mode if stakeholders want KANJ-level minimal graphics
